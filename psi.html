The Pharmaceutical Society of Ireland Equivalence Exam (PSI EE)

The Pharmaceutical Society of Ireland (PSI) â€“ The Pharmacy Regulator, is the statutory body responsible for regulating pharmacists and community pharmacies in the public interest. The Pharmaceutical Society of Ireland Equivalence Exam (PSI EE) is conducted by the PSI to offer registration to the Non-European Union trained pharmacists. Clearing the PSI Equivalence exam is the most important step to become a registered pharmacist in Ireland.
The purpose of the Equivalence Examination is to assess the standard of training, education, examinations, and qualifications undertaken by the applicant to ascertain that the standard of the applicant is not of a standard lower than that which an Irish/EU/EEA applicant must achieve in conformity with the prescribed EU Minimum Training Requirements, and the standard necessary for practice in Ireland.

Exam Schedule

The PSI exam is held biannually, taking place in April and November.

Exam Format and Content Overview

Currently the Equivalence Examination format is an online virtual examination, run over two days facilitated by an external provider on behalf of the PSI.
On Day 1, candidates need to attempt an exciting MCQ test comprising 80 questions over 150 minutes. The test is strategically divided into three sections:


  • 40 clinically-oriented pharmacology questions requiring practical knowledge.
  • 20 fundamental pharmaceutical calculations questions
  • 20 questions on basic sciences that cover essential concepts.
  • Additionally, candidates will encounter about 5 questions on pharmacy legislation, focusing on state laws related to dispensing, prescriptions, and other relevant regulations.

On Day 2, candidates will participate in both written and verbal OSCEs (Objective Structured Clinical Examinations), allowing them to demonstrate their clinical skills and knowledge in a practical setting
