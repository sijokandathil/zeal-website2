Upcoming Updates to the PEBC Evaluating Exam

Testing standards

  • Shifting from a B Pharm level to a Pharm D level
  • Effective as early as 2025 June, the exam will focus more on clinical concepts and place less emphasis on biomedical sciences.

Revised

  • The exam will be revised to include 150 questions, down from the previous total of 200.

Time

  • Students will be alloted approximately 3 hours to complete the exam.

Exam Schedule

  • Exams will be scheduled four times per year. January, March, June and October.

Examination Subject Areas


Subject Areas Portions
Biomedical Sciences (15%)
  • Biochemistry
  • Genomics and Molecular Biology
  • Physiology/ Functional Anatomy
  • Immunology
  • Medical Microbiology
Pharmaceutical Sciences (25%)
  • Pharmaceutics and drug delivery systems
  • Pharmacokinetics and Biopharmaceutics
  • Medicinal Chemistry
  • Pharmacology
  • Toxicology and Clinical Toxicology
  • Biotechnology and Pharmacogenetics
Pharmacy Practice (50%)
  • Clinical Sciences (35%)
  • Professional Practice Skills (15%)
Clinical Sciences (35%)
  • Pathophysiology
  • Clinical Biochemistry/Laboratory and Diagnostic testing
  • Pharmacotherapeutics(including Prescription, Non-prescription and Complimentary therapy)
  • Health promotion and disease prevention
  • patient care process(Assessment/Intervention/Monitoring/Follow-up/Documentation)
  • Special populations(including Geriatrics, Pediatrics, Pregnancy and Lactation
  • Nutrition
Professional Practice skills (15%)
  • Prescription Processing and Product Preparation (including Non-Sterile and Sterile Compounding)
  • Prescription calculations
  • Communication / Patient councelling
  • Drug information
  • Literature evaluation/Research methods/Evidence based decision making(including Pharmacoepidemiology)
  • Medication / Patient safety practices
  • Law / Jurisprudence
  • Professionalism / Ethics
  • Collaborative patient care
Behavioural, Social & Administrative Pharmacy Sciences (10%)
  • Pharmacy management (including Financial, Personal, Marketing, Quality improvement, Risk management and Workplace safety)
  • Canadian healthcare system
  • Pharmacoeceonomics
  • Biostatistics

Passing Status

If you acheive at least 60% of the total possible score of 150, you will be awarded a passing grade.
