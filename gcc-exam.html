The Essential MOHAP Guide

Passing the Ministry of Health and Prevention (MOHAP) exam opens up exciting career opportunities in Dubai and the northern Emirates. This certification enables pharmacists to work at healthcare centres across Sharjah, Ajman, Umm Al-Quwain, Fujairah, and Ras Al Khaimah.

Eligibility

MOHAP has stringent eligibility requirements to ensure that only skilled and qualified pharmacists receive a license to practice in the UAE. To qualify for a MOHAP license, applicants must at least hold a Bachelor of Pharmacy degree from an accredited institution and have a minimum of two years of relevant work experience. Additionally, there should be no more than a two-year gap in their employment history.

Ace the Exam

Prepare to excel in the MOHAP exam, which spans 3 hours and features around 150 multiplechoice questions. Achieving a minimum score of 70% is required to pass and secure your certification.

Test Framework

A. Pharmaceutical Sciences

1. Pharmacology
Basic principles of pharmacology including pharmacokinetics and pharmacodynamics.
Mechanism of action, therapeutic uses, and side effects of major drug classes.

2. Pharmaceutics
Fundamentals of dosage form design and drug delivery systems.
Principles of compounding and pharmaceutical calculations.

2. Medicinal Chemistry
Basic concepts in medicinal chemistry.
Chemical structure and drug activity relationship.

B. Clinical Pharmacy

1. Therapeutics
Clinical management of common diseases and pharmacotherapy.
Use of clinical guidelines in therapy planning.

2. Patient-Centered Care
Medication therapy management.
Patient counseling and education techniques.

C. Pharmacy Practice

1. Community Pharmacy
Role of the pharmacist in community settings.
Over-the-counter (OTC) medications and self-care.

2. Hospital Pharmacy
Operations and management of hospital pharmacy.
In-patient medication management and safety.

D. Pharmacokinetics and Biopharmaceutics

Principles of absorption, distribution, metabolism, and excretion of drugs.
Application of pharmacokinetic principles in clinical settings.

E. Toxicology and Poison Management

Basics of toxicology and treatment of common poisonings.
Emergency management in cases of drug overdose.

F. Pharmaceutical Jurisprudence and Ethics

Laws and regulations governing pharmacy practice in the UAE.
Ethical principles in pharmacy practice.

G. Public Health and Epidemiology

Role of pharmacists in public health.
Understanding of epidemiology and its relevance to pharmacy.

H. Drug Information and Evidence-Based Practice

Sources of drug information and their utilization.
Principles of evidence-based practice in making therapeutic decisions.

I. Pharmaceutical Care in Special Populations

Pharmacotherapy considerations in special populations such as pediatrics, geriatrics, and pregnancy.
Managing chronic diseases and polypharmacy in special populations.

J. Quality Assurance and Pharmacy Management

Principles of quality assurance in pharmacy practice.
Basics of pharmacy management including inventory, staff management, financial aspects.

DHA Pharmacist Exam

The DHA exam, short for Dubai Health Authority exam, is a mandatory assessment conducted by the Dubai Health Authority to evaluate the competency of healthcare professionals in Dubai. Specifically designed for pharmacists and pharmacy technicians, this comprehensive examination aims to ensure that individuals practicing in the field of pharmacy meet the rigorous standards set by the DHA.

Eligibility

Candidates must hold a Bachelor of Pharmacy, Master of Pharmacy, or PharmD degree, have at least two years of experience in retail or hospital pharmacy, and be a registered pharmacist in their home country.

Passing Score

The exam has a duration of 3 hours (subject to change), consists of approximately 150 multiple-choice questions (with possible slight variation), and requires a pass score of 70%.

Syllabus

The DHA Exam for pharmacists covers a wide range of topics to ensure that candidates have comprehensive knowledge and skills in their field. Here is an overview of the syllabus for pharmacists:

1. Pharmaceutical Sciences

This section includes topics such as pharmaceutics, pharmaceutical chemistry, pharmacology, OTC medications and pharmacokinetics. Candidates are expected to have a deep understanding of drug formulation, chemical properties of drugs, their effects on the body, and how they are absorbed and metabolized.

2. Clinical Pharmacy

This section focuses on the application of pharmaceutical knowledge in clinical settings. Topics include patient assessment, drug therapy monitoring, adverse drug reactions, therapeutic drug monitoring, and interactions between different medications.

3. Pharmacy Practice Management

This section covers aspects related to managing pharmacy operations effectively. It includes topics like inventory management, quality assurance programs, medication safety protocols, counseling patients on proper medication use and compliance.

4. Regulatory Guidelines

Candidates need to be familiar with various regulatory guidelines related to pharmacy practice in the UAE. Topics covered may include local laws governing pharmaceuticals distribution and dispensing practices.

5. Patient Care

This section emphasizes the importance of providing optimal care to patients by considering factors such as patient education about their medications, cultural competence when dealing with diverse populations, and adherence support strategies. Candidates should also be aware of any specific guidelines or best practices regarding special populations like pediatric or geriatric patients.

6. Pharmaceutical Markrting & Ethics

This part evaluates candidates’ understanding of ethical considerations when promoting pharmaceutical products, as well as marketing strategies used in pharmacies. It may cover topics like advertising regulations, conflict-ofinterest avoidance, and professional ethics codes

8. Pharmaceutical Calculations

9. Case Studies
