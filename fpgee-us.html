THE FOREIGN PHARMACY GRADUATE EQUIVALENCY EXAMINATION (FPGEE) for US

The Foreign Pharmacy Graduate Equivalency Examination (FPGEE) is developed and managed by the National Association of Boards of Pharmacy (NABP). This examination is used to assess the qualifications of foreign pharmacy graduates seeking FPGEC Certification. This certification is a prerequisite for pharmacists who have completed their education outside the US before they can apply for licensure. The FPGEE evaluates knowledge across four core areas: basic biomedical sciences, pharmaceutical sciences, clinical sciences, and social/behavioural/administrative sciences.

Exam Format and Duration

The FPGEE is a computerized exam consisting of 200 questions, administered over a 4.5-hour testing period.

Test Date

The exam is typically scheduled once a year in the fall, often in October. Updated dates are available on the NABP website.

Score

The minimum acceptable passing score on the FPGEE is 75.

Validity and attempts

The score is valid for 5 years from the date of examination. Applicants have a lifetime limit of five attempts to pass the exam.
